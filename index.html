The Ohio State University Consent to Participate in Research

Study Title: Eye Movements and Political Decisions

Researcher: Dr. Jason C. Coronel, Dr. Ian Krajbich, & Elizabeth Riggs

This is a consent form for research participation. It contains important information about this study and what to expect if you decide to participate.

Your participation is voluntary.

Purpose: The purpose of this study is to examine how people make voting decisions.

Procedures/Tasks: For this study, you will be asked to make decisions on a variety of statewide ballot initiatives presented on a computer screen. While you view these ballots, your eye movements will be recorded by your web camera. In addition, you will be asked to fill out a brief demographics questionnaire and a questionnaire about political knowledge.

Duration:
The study will take 60 minutes to complete. You may leave the study at any time. If you decide to stop participating in the study, there will be no penalty to you, and you will not lose any benefits to which you are otherwise entitled. Your decision will not affect your future relationship with The Ohio State University.

Risks and Benefits:
This study does not involve any more than minimal risk to the participant. In other words, there are no harms or discomforts beyond what is ordinarily encountered in daily life or during the performance of routine physical or psychological tests.

You may benefit by gaining some knowledge about psychological research, both from the experience of participating and from the debriefing period following each session where the hypothesis of the study will be explained.

Confidentiality:
Once the data collection for this study is completed, the researchers will destroy they link between your name and the data you contributed to the study. Following these procedures, there would then be no way to connect your answers back to you personally.

The only record of your name that will be kept is the fact that you participated in this study, but this will no longer be connected to the answers you gave.

Efforts will be made to keep your study-related information confidential. However, there may be circumstances where this information must be released. For example, personal information regarding your participation in this study may be disclosed if required by state law. Also, your records may be reviewed by the following groups:

  • Office for Human Research Protections or other federal, state, or international regulatory agencies
  • The Ohio State University Institutional Review Board or Office of Responsible Research Practices

Future Research:
Your de-identified information may be used or shared with other researchers without your additional informed consent.

Incentives:
This study is part of the School of Communication research participant pool program (C-REP).  Students enrolled in COMM 1100 and 1101 are required to participate in either research or alternative writing assignments for credit.  Students enrolled in other communication courses are permitted to participate for extra credit as well, per their instructor and syllabus. Your participation in the study qualifies for 1 credit in the C-REP. You are free to withdraw from the study at any time.

Participant Rights:
You may refuse to participate in this study without penalty or loss of benefits to which you are otherwise entitled. If you are a student or employee at Ohio State, your decision will not affect your grades or employment status.

If you choose to participate in the study, you may discontinue participation at any time without penalty or loss of benefits. You may also choose to withdraw your data up to two weeks after participation by contacting the researcher, Jason C. Coronel, at coronel.4@osu.edu or (614) 292-9062. By signing this form, you do not give up any personal legal rights you may have as a participant in this study.

An Institutional Review Board responsible for human subjects research at The Ohio State University reviewed this research project and found it to be acceptable, according to applicable state and federal regulations and University policies designed to protect the rights and welfare of participants in research.

Contacts and Questions:
For questions, concerns, or complaints about the study, or you feel you have been harmed as a result of study participation, you may contact Jason C. Coronel. He can be reached by email at coronel.4@osu.edu or by phone at (614) 292-9062.

For questions about your rights as a participant in this study or to discuss other study-related concerns or complaints with someone who is not part of the research team, you may contact Ms. Sandra Meadows in the Office of Responsible Research Practices at 1-800-678-6251.
